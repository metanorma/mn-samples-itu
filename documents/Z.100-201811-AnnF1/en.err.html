<clause id="abstract_state_machines" inline-header="false" obligation="normative">
<title>Abstract State Machines</title>
<p id="_71b0aacc-b73b-a79d-320d-49c08ecdc7e4">This clause explains the basic notions and concepts of <em>Abstract State Machines</em> (<em>ASM</em>) as well as the notation used in these Annexes F1, F2 and F3 to define the SDL-2010 abstract machine model. The objective here is to provide an intuitive understanding of the formalism; for a rigorous definition of the mathematical foundations of ASM and its application, the reader is referred to <eref type="inline" style="short" bibitemid="b-Gurevich" citeas="b-Gurevich"/>, <eref type="inline" style="short" bibitemid="b-Blass" citeas="b-Blass"/>, <eref type="inline" style="short" bibitemid="b-Blass_2008" citeas="b-Blass 2008"/>, <eref type="inline" style="short" bibitemid="b-Glausch" citeas="b-Glausch"/>, <eref type="inline" style="short" bibitemid="b-BoergerStaerk" citeas="b-Börger  Stärk"/> and <eref type="inline" style="short" bibitemid="b-Boerger" citeas="b-Börger"/>. A discussion and motivation of the appropriateness of the semantic framework used here is given in <eref type="inline" style="short" bibitemid="b-Eschbach" citeas="b-Eschbach"/>, <eref type="inline" style="short" bibitemid="b-Eschbach_2001" citeas="b-Eschbach 2001"/> and <eref type="inline" style="short" bibitemid="b-Glaesser" citeas="b-Glässer"/>. Further references on ASM-related material can also be found on the ASM webpages <eref type="inline" style="short" bibitemid="b-ASM" citeas="b-ASM"/>.</p>

<p id="_6d2ab0f9-90e0-4921-e009-06c9f58dd107">The ASM model used to define the dynamic semantics of SDL-2010 is explained in several steps. Firstly, the <em>basic ASM model</em> with a single agent is treated (see <xref target="basic_asm_model" style="short"/>). Next, this model is extended to cover <em>multi-agent systems</em> (see <xref target="distributed_multi_agent_asm" style="short"/>). Then, <em>open systems</em>, i.e., systems interacting with an environment they cannot control, are addressed by adding the notion of <em>external world</em> (see <xref target="the_external_world" style="short"/>). Finally, the model is extended by introducing a notion of <em>real-time behaviour</em> (see <xref target="real_time_behaviour" style="short"/>). To illustrate these steps, an ASM model for a simple system is developed, step-by-step. The final ASM model of this system is summarized in <xref target="example_the_system_rms" style="short"/>. Additional notation used to define the dynamic semantics of SDL-2010 is explained in <xref target="predefined_names_and_special_symbols" style="short"/>.</p>
